🚢 Seasickness Ladder — Start Gentle, Escalate if Needed

1
Ginger chews — anytime, no side effects, good for mild queasiness
2
Bonine (meclizine, non-drowsy) — take the night before boarding (Apr 5 evening) and continue as needed
3
Dramamine Original (drowsy) — step up if Bonine isn't enough; plan around it causing sleepiness
4
Dramamine Naturals (ginger-based) — gentlest pharmaceutical option, good daytime backup

⚠️ Roughest conditions: Cape Horn (Day 6) and Strait of Magellan. Days 5–8 are all at sea.

Bonine (Meclizine)
Seasickness · Non-Drowsy
⏰ Take: Night of Apr 5 (before boarding Apr 6), continue nightly
Non-drowsy antihistamine. Take once daily. Works best when taken preventively — don't wait until you feel sick.
💡 This is your primary seasickness defense. Start it the evening before you board.
Dramamine Original
Seasickness · Drowsy
⏰ Take: If Bonine alone isn't working; plan for drowsiness
Stronger than Bonine but causes significant drowsiness. Don't combine with alcohol. Best taken when you can rest.
Dramamine Naturals
Seasickness · Ginger-Based
⏰ Take: As gentler daytime alternative or supplement
Ginger-based, minimal side effects. Can be used alongside or instead of Bonine on milder sea days.
Ibuprofen
Pain / Anti-Inflammatory
⏰ Take: As needed for muscle soreness, headache, inflammation
Standard dosing. Good for next-day muscle soreness after demanding hikes (Days 6, 7, 10, 11). Take with food.
Imodium (Loperamide)
GI · Anti-Diarrheal
⏰ Take: At first sign of diarrhea — especially if you have activities that day
Slows gut motility. Use for acute diarrhea, especially before long bus rides or active days. Follow package dosing. Note: also in your blue travel kit (DiaResQ).
⚠️ If you have fever or bloody stool, don't use Imodium — use Azithromycin instead and seek care.
Azithromycin (Z-Pack)
Antibiotic · Prescription
⏰ Take: Only if severe traveler's diarrhea (6+ loose stools/day, fever, or blood)
Broad-spectrum antibiotic prescribed by travel clinic. This is your "big gun" — don't use for mild GI upset. Complete the full course if you start it.
⚠️ Save this for genuine need. Consult a doctor if possible before starting. Remote locations means no nearby medical facilities Days 5–12.
Blue Travel Kit
Travel Clinic Kit
⏰ Use: As needed throughout trip
Contents: AssureGI (digestive protection), DiaResQ (diarrhea relief), Imodium, water purification tablets, Trioral rehydration salts, Pepto Bismol, Tums, laxatives, measuring syringe, antiseptic towelettes.
💡 Trioral rehydration salts — use if you have diarrhea or are very dehydrated. Mix with clean water.
Allegra (Fexofenadine)
Daily · Allergy
⏰ Take: Every day, same as home — don't skip
Your daily antihistamine. Continue throughout entire trip including travel days. Patagonia has strong winds that kick up dust and pollen; Buenos Aires is a major city with urban allergens.
Melatonin
Sleep · JFK → Buenos Aires Flight
⏰ Take: ~30 min before you want to fall asleep on the plane
JFK to Buenos Aires is roughly 11–12 hours overnight. Take melatonin once you're settled and the cabin lights are dimmed. Standard dose 0.5–3mg is usually enough — more isn't better. Also use for jet lag adjustment first 1–2 nights.
💡 Pair with eye mask + neck pillow. Don't look at your phone screen after taking it. Buenos Aires is only 1 hour ahead of ET so jet lag will be minimal.
Magnesium Glycinate
Sleep Support
⏰ Take: With melatonin on flight, and on restless nights during trip
Magnesium glycinate is well-absorbed and calming without side effects. Works synergistically with melatonin — the combination is more effective than either alone for sleep onset. Also helpful for muscle recovery after demanding hikes.
💡 On high-activity days (Days 6, 7, 10, 11), taking MG glycinate at night can help with both sleep quality and next-day muscle soreness.
Hydration
⏰ Take: On long hike days, bus days, or when you feel run down
Mix one stick into 16oz water. Great for Days 10–12 (heavy hiking), Day 12 (12hr bus), and any day with significant physical activity. Mandarin orange flavor.

✈️ JFK → Buenos Aires Flight Sleep Strategy

~11–12 hour overnight flight. Buenos Aires is only 1hr ahead of ET so jet lag will be minimal on arrival — the main goal is just arriving rested.

  • Take melatonin (0.5–3mg) + magnesium glycinate ~30min before you want to sleep
  • Eye mask + neck pillow matter more than anything — bring both in your carry-on
  • Avoid phone screen after taking melatonin
  • Eat light on the plane — heavy meals make sleep harder
  • Limit alcohol — dehydrating at altitude and disrupts sleep quality
  • Ask for a window seat if possible — something to lean against

🚢 Managing Seasickness — Practical Tips

  • Start Bonine the night before boarding (Apr 5) — don't wait until you feel sick
  • Stay on deck in fresh air — going below to a closed cabin makes it much worse
  • Keep your eyes on the horizon, not on your phone or a book
  • Eat light but don't be on an empty stomach — crackers, bread work well
  • Midship and lower decks have the least motion
  • Ginger chews work surprisingly well as a first-line option
  • The crew does this route constantly — they know exactly what's coming
  • Cape Horn landings are weather-dependent — they won't send you if it's unsafe

Patagonia (Days 5–13): 50–60°F, wind up to 44mph with gusts to 56mph, rain possible anytime. Layer aggressively — it changes fast.

Buenos Aires (Days 2–3, 14–15): Daily highs in the 80s. Much warmer — pack a separate layer set.

Iguassu Falls (Days 16–18): 90°F+ with 90% humidity. Completely different climate. You'll get wet from the falls — bring a dry bag for your phone.

🚢 Life on the Ventus Australis (Days 5–8)

  • 210-passenger small ship — not a massive cruise liner
  • All excursions via Zodiac inflatable boats — requires balance and agility to board
  • Shore landings are weather-dependent — Cape Horn especially may be skipped
  • No elevator on ship
  • Attire always casual — no need to dress up for dinner
  • Dinners start late by American standards
  • Complimentary beverages available throughout voyage
  • Lecture series on glaciology, history, wildlife in ship's lounge

🥾 Physical Demands
